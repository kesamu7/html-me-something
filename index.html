A trip to Guatemala.

This past August, I travelled to the country where I was born, Guatemala. I have been back many times,however this trip was different and special because it would be my father and only. Normally my whole family goes together.

If you would like to learn more about Guatemala, click on the flag below.

I was born in Guatemala City,Guatemala. My father received a request from his company in Guatemala, they were asking if he wanted to come to the United States to work. He accepted the offer and we moved here. I became an American citizen when I was 15 years old. I love the United States, but I also like travelling back to Guatemala every chance I get. In August I travelled to Guatemala with my father. I will be sharing some pictures and will provide detail about every picture.

A coffee plantation

We have some friends in Guatemala who own a coffee plantation. They invited us to come see the workings of the plantation. We saw how coffee is harvested and transported to coffee processing locations.

Fidel Castro's old house in Guatemala.

My father and I were surprised to find out that Fidel Castro once live in Guatemala. A friend of ours took us to a small town where Fidel Castro hid from the Cuban government before going back to Cuba to start a revolution. We met a man at this location that told us many stories about Castro, and about how he would speak of starting a revolution in Cuba someday.

I love lychee!!!!

My new favorite fruit in the world is lychee. I had heard of lychee before but had not attempted to taste it. Some of my friends and family have had me taste lychee ice cream. I liked it, but I had no idea what lychee looked like or what it even looked like.

My favorite city in the world.

Everytime I go to Guatemala. I make time, so that I may travel to a city named Antigua. It has a long history. It was built in the 1500's by Spanish conquistadors. It is a beautiful city with cobblestone streets and all the buildings have the typical architecture that was used in Spain during the 1500's.
